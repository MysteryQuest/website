Methodology

How We Separate Fact from Fiction

Core Principles

Evidence-Based

All claims must be supported by verifiable evidence from credible sources.

Transparent

We show our work, cite sources, and acknowledge uncertainty.

Objective

We follow evidence wherever it leads, without preconceived conclusions.

Credibility Scoring System

Each source receives a credibility score (0-100) based on multiple factors:

Source Authority Weight: 30%

Academic journals, government agencies, and established news organizations score higher than anonymous blogs or social media posts.

Track Record Weight: 25%

Historical accuracy, corrections policy, and peer review processes are evaluated.

Verifiability Weight: 25%

Can the information be independently verified? Are original sources cited?

Bias Assessment Weight: 20%

Potential conflicts of interest, political leanings, and sensationalism are factored.

Score Interpretation

80-100: Highly Credible

Peer-reviewed, authoritative sources

50-79: Moderately Credible

Established but potentially biased

0-49: Low Credibility

Unverified or questionable sources

Investigation Workflow

1. Initial Triage

  • Identify core claims and assertions
  • Determine verifiability potential
  • Assess available evidence and sources
  • Establish investigation scope and timeline

2. Data Collection

  • OSINT tools deployed for public data gathering
  • Cross-reference multiple independent sources
  • Archive all evidence with timestamps
  • Document methodology for reproducibility

3. AI Analysis

  • Pattern recognition and anomaly detection
  • Sentiment analysis of witness accounts
  • Image/video forensic analysis
  • Statistical correlation analysis

4. Human Verification

  • Expert review of AI findings
  • Community fact-checking and peer review
  • Devil's advocate analysis
  • Alternative explanation evaluation

5. Publication

  • D-LOG report drafted with full sourcing
  • Confidence levels assigned to conclusions
  • Limitations and uncertainties disclosed
  • Community voting and feedback enabled

Quality Standards

We Commit To:

  • Correcting errors promptly and transparently
  • Citing all sources with verification links
  • Disclosing conflicts of interest
  • Respecting privacy and avoiding doxing
  • Maintaining editorial independence

We Avoid:

  • Confirmation bias and cherry-picking data
  • Sensationalism and clickbait tactics
  • Anonymous or unverifiable sources
  • Jumping to conclusions without evidence
  • Ad hominem attacks and character assassination
